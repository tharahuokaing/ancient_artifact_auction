Rim & Neck: Outward-projecting, thick, heavy collar rim with a pronounced under-ridge. Surface Treatment: Displays prominent vertical slip-drip or glaze-run patterns cascading down the upper midsection. This is a classic indicator of a rapid, rustic production style where an iron-rich slip or simple glaze wash was quickly poured or wiped over the shoulder before firing. Surface Condition & Preservation: Wear Patina: The body shows deep environmental weathering, characterized by an orange-terracotta hue on the lower left base contrasting with a dark, ash-shadowed grey patina across the midsection. Damages & Markings: A prominent vertical hairline surface fracture or deep scratch is visible on the upper shoulder near a small, light-colored patch of clay loss. The lower base rim shows typical minor chipping and erosion from sitting directly on stone or earth floors.

2. Estimate of Origin and Year : This shape and firing profile are highly characteristic of regional, decentralized kiln complexes producing utilitarian household wares. Estimated Origin: Lower Mekong Basin / Mainland Southeast Asia (local Cambodian provincial or Thai/Burmese border kilns). Estimated Period: 17th Century to Early 19th Century (Late Post-Angkor / Oudong Period). Context: The barrel-like silhouette, heavy wide-lip rim, and deliberate iron-wash drippings are indicative of classic regional fermentation crocks used for storing liquids, pickling vegetables, or curing fish pastes (prahok). The profound mineral absorption on the ceramic body points to several centuries of domestic use and subsequent outdoor exposure.

Evaluation Recommendation: This item represents an excellent piece of regional ceramic heritage. Its distinct vertical markings make it a compelling item for auction catalogs—be sure to highlight the "organic slip-drip patina" in the marketing headers.
